Scope and politics

Estudos de literatura brasileira contemporânea accepts articles on its theme, including the comparative perspective, with no restrictions to theoretical or methodological approaches. The journal presents three distinct fixed sections: one of them in a given relevant issue in the area (thematic section), one for varied articles (open theme section), and the last one on reviews.

All texts, with no exception, will be sent for peer review, and the mutual anonymity will be kept.

The authors of approved works agree to relinquish non exclusive rights of publication, remaining free to release the texts on other means provided they mention the publishing of the first version on the Journal. They also authorize the journal to relinquish its contents for reproduction in virtual libraries, content indexers and the like. The authors assume that the texts submitted for publication are their personal and original production, taking full responsibility of the content in any possible impugnation by any third part. The authors declare, as well, there is no conflict of interests regarded to the published text.

 

Form and preparation of manuscripts

Authors are asked to submit manuscripts that have not been published or simultaneously submitted for publishing elsewhere. The articles must be sent in file format DOC or docx, Time New Roman 12, 1.5 space. The bibliographic cross-references should appear in the system author-date, in parentheses, in the midst of the text, as in the model: (Bourdieu, 1998, p. 37) Other information should come in footnotes. The full references should appear at the end of the text, in the following order: surname of the author in upper case, first name starting with a capital letter, year of publication in parentheses, book title, edition, city and publisher. Articles should have at least 10 pages and not more than 20 pages. An abstract and three or four keywords should also be included. The title, abstract and keywords must be submitted in the following languages: Portuguese, English and Spanish. Some information about the author’s background and affiliation, and an e-mail that could be disclosed are required.

The reviews on fictional or theoretical texts should not exceed five pages and should deal with books published in the last 24 months. Only reviews which proposes a critical analysis will be accepted, as we expect reviews to be more than just a simple summary of the plot. The same guidelines regarding the format of articles should be followed in the reviews.

The journal publishes texts preferably in Portuguese, but submissions in Spanish, English, Italian and French can also be accepted. Articles in other languages can be submitted to the evaluation of the editorial board and, if approved, will be published in the original language or in translation (the costs of the translation should be covered by the author).

Due to the high number of submissions received and to its outstanding evaluation by Capes as Qualis A1, the Journal Estudos de literatura brasileira contemporânea only accepts articles by authors with doctorate completed or in progress.
